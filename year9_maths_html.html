Year 9 Mathematics

Non-Calculator Exam Topics - Detailed Study Guide

About This Guide

Purpose: This comprehensive breakdown covers all key topics for Year 9 non-calculator mathematics examinations in the UK education system.

Usage: Each topic can be developed into focused study guides with worked examples and practice problems.

Level: Suitable for Year 9 students (ages 13-14) following the UK National Curriculum.

1. Substitution

  • Substituting positive and negative integers into algebraic expressions
  • Substituting fractions and decimals into formulae
  • Evaluating expressions with multiple variables
  • Using substitution to check solutions to equations
  • Substituting into geometric formulae (perimeter, area, volume)

2. Solving Number Problems in Words

  • Setting up equations from written descriptions
  • Age problems and their algebraic representation
  • Money and cost problems
  • Percentage increase and decrease problems
  • Ratio and proportion word problems
  • Consecutive number problems
  • Problems involving multiplicative relationships

3. Simplifying Ratios

  • Writing ratios in simplest form
  • Equivalent ratios and scaling up/down
  • Ratios with different units (converting first)
  • Three-part ratios and their simplification
  • Ratios involving decimals and fractions
  • Using ratios to solve sharing problems
  • Understanding ratio as a multiplicative relationship

4. Solving Linear Equations

  • Linear equations with one unknown (including brackets)
  • Equations with the unknown on both sides
  • Equations involving fractions
  • Solving equations with negative solutions
  • Checking solutions by substitution
  • Forming and solving equations from geometric problems
  • Understanding what makes a solution valid

5. "I'm Thinking of a Number" Problems

  • Translating verbal descriptions into algebraic equations
  • Working backwards from given information
  • Multi-step number puzzles
  • Problems involving operations in sequence
  • Using inverse operations to solve

6. Area of Compound Shapes

  • Breaking complex shapes into rectangles and triangles
  • Finding areas by addition and subtraction methods
  • Areas involving trapeziums and parallelograms
  • Missing length problems in compound shapes
  • Area of circles and sectors
  • Real-world applications (flooring, painting, etc.)

7. Scatter Graphs

  • Plotting points accurately on coordinate grids
  • Identifying correlation (positive, negative, none)
  • Drawing lines of best fit by eye
  • Using lines of best fit to make predictions
  • Identifying outliers and their significance
  • Interpreting scatter graphs in context

8. Two-Way Tables

  • Completing frequency tables with missing values
  • Reading and interpreting data from tables
  • Finding totals and calculating missing entries
  • Using two-way tables to answer probability questions
  • Converting between frequencies and percentages

9. Enlargement Through a Center

  • Identifying scale factors from diagrams
  • Finding centers of enlargement
  • Performing enlargements with positive scale factors
  • Fractional scale factors (making shapes smaller)
  • Understanding similarity and scale factors
  • Finding missing coordinates after enlargement

10. Using Estimations

  • Rounding to 1 significant figure for quick calculations
  • Estimating answers to check reasonableness
  • Using approximations for calculations
  • Estimating areas and volumes
  • Order of magnitude problems
  • Checking calculator work with estimates

11. Expanding Single and Double Brackets

  • Expanding expressions like 3(x + 4)
  • Expanding with negative terms: -2(3x - 5)
  • Expanding and simplifying: 4(x + 3) + 2(x - 1)
  • Double brackets: (x + 3)(x + 5)
  • Expanding (x + a)(x + b) where a and b can be negative
  • Products of binomials

12. Factorising

  • Finding common factors from algebraic expressions
  • Factorising expressions with numerical coefficients
  • Factorising quadratic expressions (simple cases)
  • Factorising by grouping (four terms)
  • Factorising the difference of two squares
  • Using factorising to solve simple quadratic equations

13. Solving Simultaneous Linear Equations by Elimination

  • Adding equations to eliminate one variable
  • Subtracting equations to eliminate one variable
  • Multiplying equations before elimination
  • Solving systems where coefficients need adjustment
  • Checking solutions by substitution
  • Setting up simultaneous equations from word problems

Study Guide Recommendations

For each topic, include:

  • Key concepts and definitions
  • Step-by-step worked examples
  • 8-10 practice problems with increasing difficulty
  • Space for working (at least 5cm between problems)
  • Extension problems for stronger students
  • Common mistakes section
  • Quick reference formulas where applicable

Page Layout Suggestions:

  • A4 pages with wide margins (2cm minimum)
  • Large, clear fonts (minimum 12pt)
  • Plenty of white space between problems
  • Grid paper sections for graph work
  • Answer spaces clearly marked
  • Print on one side only for maximum workspace
