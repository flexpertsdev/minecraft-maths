Topic 1: Substitution

Practice Problems & Detailed Answer Guide

Key Concepts to Remember

Substitution means replacing letters (variables) with given numbers and then calculating the result.

Order of operations (BIDMAS/BODMAS): Brackets, Indices/Orders, Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction

Key point: Always substitute first, then follow the order of operations

Problem 1
If x = 4 and y = -2, find the value of:
a) 3x + 2y
b) x² - y
c) 2(x + y)
Problem 2
If a = 5, b = -3, and c = 2, find the value of:

c) (a - b) ÷ c
Problem 3

Find the area when b = 8 cm and h = 6 cm.
Problem 4
If p = 2.5 and q = 0.4, find the value of:
a) 4p - 10q
b) pq + p²
c) (p + q) × 6
Problem 5
The perimeter of a rectangle is given by P = 2(l + w), where l is length and w is width.
Find the perimeter when l = 12.5 cm and w = 7.8 cm.
Problem 6
If m = -4, n = 3, and k = -1, find the value of:
a) m² + n²
b) 2m - 3n + k

Problem 7

Problem 8
If x = ½ and y = ¼, find the value of:

Detailed Answer Guide

Complete explanations showing WHY and HOW

Answer 1
Given: x = 4, y = -2
a) 3x + 2y
Step 1: Substitute the values: 3(4) + 2(-2)
Step 2: Multiply: 12 + (-4)
Step 3: Add: 12 - 4 = 8
Answer: 8
b) x² - y
Step 1: Substitute the values: (4)² - (-2)
Step 2: Calculate the square: 16 - (-2)
Step 3: Subtract the negative (which means add): 16 + 2 = 18
Answer: 18
c) 2(x + y)
Step 1: Substitute the values: 2(4 + (-2))
Step 2: Work inside brackets first: 2(4 - 2) = 2(2)
Step 3: Multiply: 2 × 2 = 4
Answer: 4

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

• Don't forget to use brackets when substituting negative numbers
• Remember that x² means x × x, not 2x
• Always do brackets first (BIDMAS)
Answer 2
Given: a = 5, b = -3, c = 2
a) a + 2b - c
Step 1: Substitute: 5 + 2(-3) - 2
Step 2: Multiply first: 5 + (-6) - 2
Step 3: Work left to right: 5 - 6 - 2 = -1 - 2 = -3
Answer: -3
b) ab + c²
Step 1: Substitute: (5)(-3) + (2)²
Step 2: Calculate: -15 + 4
Step 3: Add: -15 + 4 = -11
Answer: -11
c) (a - b) ÷ c
Step 1: Substitute: (5 - (-3)) ÷ 2
Step 2: Brackets first: (5 + 3) ÷ 2 = 8 ÷ 2
Step 3: Divide: 8 ÷ 2 = 4
Answer: 4
Answer 3
Given formula: A = ½bh, where b = 8 cm, h = 6 cm
Step 1: Substitute the values: A = ½ × 8 × 6
Step 2: Multiply: A = ½ × 48
Step 3: Calculate: A = 48 ÷ 2 = 24
Answer: 24 cm²

Why this works: The formula ½bh comes from the fact that a triangle is half the area of a rectangle with the same base and height. So we find the area of the rectangle (8 × 6 = 48) and then halve it.

Answer 4
Given: p = 2.5, q = 0.4
a) 4p - 10q
Step 1: Substitute: 4(2.5) - 10(0.4)
Step 2: Multiply: 10 - 4
Step 3: Subtract: 10 - 4 = 6
Answer: 6
b) pq + p²
Step 1: Substitute: (2.5)(0.4) + (2.5)²
Step 2: Calculate: 1 + 6.25
Step 3: Add: 1 + 6.25 = 7.25
Answer: 7.25
c) (p + q) × 6
Step 1: Substitute: (2.5 + 0.4) × 6
Step 2: Brackets first: 2.9 × 6
Step 3: Multiply: 2.9 × 6 = 17.4
Answer: 17.4
Answer 5
Given formula: P = 2(l + w), where l = 12.5 cm, w = 7.8 cm
Step 1: Substitute: P = 2(12.5 + 7.8)
Step 2: Brackets first: P = 2(20.3)
Step 3: Multiply: P = 2 × 20.3 = 40.6
Answer: 40.6 cm

Understanding the formula: P = 2(l + w) works because a rectangle has two lengths and two widths. So the perimeter is length + width + length + width = 2(length + width).

Answer 6
Given: m = -4, n = 3, k = -1
a) m² + n²
Step 1: Substitute: (-4)² + (3)²
Step 2: Calculate squares: 16 + 9
Step 3: Add: 16 + 9 = 25
Answer: 25

Remember: (-4)² = (-4) × (-4) = +16 (negative × negative = positive)

b) 2m - 3n + k
Step 1: Substitute: 2(-4) - 3(3) + (-1)
Step 2: Multiply: -8 - 9 + (-1)
Step 3: Calculate left to right: -8 - 9 - 1 = -18
Answer: -18
c) mn ÷ k
Step 1: Substitute: (-4)(3) ÷ (-1)
Step 2: Multiply first: -12 ÷ (-1)
Step 3: Divide: -12 ÷ (-1) = +12
Answer: 12

Remember: negative ÷ negative = positive

Answer 7
Check if x = 3 is a solution to 2x + 5 = 11
Step 1: Substitute x = 3 into the left side: 2(3) + 5
Step 2: Calculate: 6 + 5 = 11
Step 3: Compare with right side: 11 = 11 ✓
Yes, x = 3 IS a solution because both sides equal 11

How to check solutions: Always substitute the given value into the original equation. If both sides are equal, it's a solution. If not, it's not a solution.

Answer 8
Given: x = ½, y = ¼
a) 8x + 4y
Step 1: Substitute: 8(½) + 4(¼)
Step 2: Multiply: 4 + 1
Step 3: Add: 4 + 1 = 5
Answer: 5
b) xy
Step 1: Substitute: (½)(¼)
Step 2: Multiply fractions: ½ × ¼ = ⅛
Answer: ⅛ or 0.125
c) x ÷ y
Step 1: Substitute: ½ ÷ ¼
Step 2: Divide fractions (multiply by reciprocal): ½ × 4/1
Step 3: Calculate: ½ × 4 = 2
Answer: 2
