Topic 2: Solving Number Problems in Words

Practice Problems & Detailed Answer Guide

Key Concepts to Remember

Word problems require you to translate words into mathematical expressions and equations.

Key phrases: "more than" → +, "less than" → -, "times" → ×, "of" → ×, "is" → =

Strategy: Define your variable, write the equation, solve, check your answer makes sense

Problem 1
Sarah thinks of a number. She multiplies it by 3 and then adds 7. Her answer is 25.
What number did Sarah think of?
Problem 2
The sum of two consecutive numbers is 47. Find the two numbers.
Problem 3
A rectangle has a length that is 4 cm more than its width. If the perimeter is 28 cm, find the length and width.
Problem 4
Tom is 3 years older than his sister. The sum of their ages is 27. How old are Tom and his sister?
Problem 5
A shop increases all prices by 20%. If a jacket now costs £36, what was the original price?
Problem 6
The cost of 5 pens and 3 pencils is £4.20. If each pen costs 60p, how much does each pencil cost?
Problem 7
A number is doubled and then 15 is subtracted. The result is the same as the original number plus 8. Find the number.
Problem 8
Three consecutive odd numbers have a sum of 75. Find the three numbers.

Detailed Answer Guide - Topic 2

Answer 1
Step 1: Define the variable. Let x = the number Sarah thinks of
Step 2: Translate to algebra: "multiply by 3 then add 7" = 3x + 7
Step 3: "Her answer is 25" means: 3x + 7 = 25
Step 4: Solve: 3x = 25 - 7 = 18
Step 5: Therefore x = 18 ÷ 3 = 6
Step 6: Check: 3(6) + 7 = 18 + 7 = 25 ✓
Sarah thought of the number 6
Answer 2
Step 1: Let x = the first number
Step 2: Consecutive numbers differ by 1, so second number = x + 1
Step 3: "Sum is 47" means: x + (x + 1) = 47
Step 4: Simplify: 2x + 1 = 47
Step 5: Solve: 2x = 46, so x = 23
Step 6: Second number = 23 + 1 = 24
Step 7: Check: 23 + 24 = 47 ✓
The two consecutive numbers are 23 and 24
Answer 3
Step 1: Let w = width of rectangle
Step 2: "Length is 4 cm more than width" means length = w + 4
Step 3: Perimeter = 2(length + width) = 2(w + 4 + w) = 2(2w + 4)
Step 4: "Perimeter is 28 cm" means: 2(2w + 4) = 28
Step 5: Simplify: 4w + 8 = 28
Step 6: Solve: 4w = 20, so w = 5 cm
Step 7: Length = w + 4 = 5 + 4 = 9 cm
Step 8: Check: Perimeter = 2(9 + 5) = 2(14) = 28 ✓
Width = 5 cm, Length = 9 cm
Answer 4
Step 1: Let s = sister's age
Step 2: "Tom is 3 years older" means Tom's age = s + 3
Step 3: "Sum of ages is 27" means: s + (s + 3) = 27
Step 4: Simplify: 2s + 3 = 27
Step 5: Solve: 2s = 24, so s = 12
Step 6: Tom's age = s + 3 = 12 + 3 = 15
Step 7: Check: 12 + 15 = 27 ✓
Sister is 12 years old, Tom is 15 years old
Answer 5
Step 1: Let x = original price
Step 2: "20% increase" means new price = x + 0.2x = 1.2x
Step 3: "Now costs £36" means: 1.2x = 36
Step 4: Solve: x = 36 ÷ 1.2 = 30
Step 5: Check: 20% of £30 = £6, so new price = £30 + £6 = £36 ✓
The original price was £30

Alternative method: If the new price is 120% of the original, then £36 represents 120%. So 1% = £36 ÷ 120 = £0.30, and 100% = £30.

Answer 6
Step 1: Let p = cost of each pencil (in pence)
Step 2: Cost of 5 pens = 5 × 60p = 300p
Step 3: Cost of 3 pencils = 3p
Step 4: Total cost = 300p + 3p = £4.20 = 420p
Step 5: So: 300 + 3p = 420
Step 6: Solve: 3p = 420 - 300 = 120
Step 7: Therefore: p = 120 ÷ 3 = 40p
Step 8: Check: 5 × 60p + 3 × 40p = 300p + 120p = 420p = £4.20 ✓
Each pencil costs 40p
Answer 7
Step 1: Let x = the original number
Step 2: "Doubled then subtract 15" = 2x - 15
Step 3: "Original number plus 8" = x + 8
Step 4: "The result is the same" means: 2x - 15 = x + 8
Step 5: Solve: 2x - x = 8 + 15
Step 6: Therefore: x = 23
Step 7: Check: 2(23) - 15 = 46 - 15 = 31, and 23 + 8 = 31 ✓
The number is 23
Answer 8
Step 1: Let x = the first odd number
Step 2: Consecutive odd numbers differ by 2, so the three numbers are: x, x+2, x+4
Step 3: "Sum is 75" means: x + (x + 2) + (x + 4) = 75
Step 4: Simplify: 3x + 6 = 75
Step 5: Solve: 3x = 69, so x = 23
Step 6: The three numbers are: 23, 25, 27
Step 7: Check: 23 + 25 + 27 = 75 ✓ (and all are consecutive odd numbers ✓)
The three consecutive odd numbers are 23, 25, and 27

Topic 3: Simplifying Ratios

Practice Problems & Detailed Answer Guide

Key Concepts to Remember

Ratio compares quantities by division. Written as a:b or a/b

Simplifying: Divide both parts by their highest common factor (HCF)

Equivalent ratios: Multiply or divide both parts by the same number

Units: Always convert to the same units before simplifying

Problem 1
Simplify these ratios to their lowest terms:
a) 12:8
b) 15:25
c) 24:36:48
Problem 2
Express these ratios in their simplest form:
a) 2.4:1.6
b) ¾:½
c) 0.6:0.9:1.2
Problem 3
Convert to the same units, then simplify:
a) 2 hours : 45 minutes
b) 1.5 kg : 300 g
c) 80 cm : 1.2 m
Problem 4
A recipe uses flour and sugar in the ratio 4:3. If I use 200g of flour, how much sugar do I need?
Problem 5
£60 is shared between Anna, Ben and Charlie in the ratio 2:3:5. How much does each person get?
Problem 6
The angles in a triangle are in the ratio 2:3:4. Find the size of each angle.
Problem 7
In a class of 28 students, the ratio of boys to girls is 3:4. How many boys and girls are there?
Problem 8
A paint mixture uses red, blue and yellow paint in the ratio 5:3:2. How much of each color is needed to make 2 litres of paint?

Detailed Answer Guide - Topic 3

Answer 1
a) 12:8
Step 1: Find the HCF of 12 and 8
Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Factors of 8: 1, 2, 4, 8
HCF = 4
Step 2: Divide both parts by 4: 12÷4 : 8÷4 = 3:2
12:8 = 3:2
b) 15:25
HCF of 15 and 25 = 5
15÷5 : 25÷5 = 3:5
15:25 = 3:5
c) 24:36:48
HCF of 24, 36, and 48 = 12
24÷12 : 36÷12 : 48÷12 = 2:3:4
24:36:48 = 2:3:4
Answer 2
a) 2.4:1.6
Step 1: Multiply both parts by 10 to remove decimals: 24:16
Step 2: Find HCF of 24 and 16 = 8
Step 3: 24÷8 : 16÷8 = 3:2
2.4:1.6 = 3:2
b) ¾:½
Step 1: Convert to same denominator: ¾ = 3/4, ½ = 2/4
Step 2: Ratio of numerators: 3:2
¾:½ = 3:2
c) 0.6:0.9:1.2
Step 1: Multiply by 10: 6:9:12
Step 2: HCF of 6, 9, 12 = 3
Step 3: 6÷3 : 9÷3 : 12÷3 = 2:3:4
0.6:0.9:1.2 = 2:3:4
Answer 3
a) 2 hours : 45 minutes
Step 1: Convert to same units: 2 hours = 120 minutes
Step 2: Ratio becomes 120:45
Step 3: HCF of 120 and 45 = 15
Step 4: 120÷15 : 45÷15 = 8:3
2 hours : 45 minutes = 8:3
b) 1.5 kg : 300 g
Step 1: Convert to grams: 1.5 kg = 1500 g
Step 2: Ratio becomes 1500:300
Step 3: Divide by 300: 1500÷300 : 300÷300 = 5:1
1.5 kg : 300 g = 5:1
c) 80 cm : 1.2 m
Step 1: Convert to cm: 1.2 m = 120 cm
Step 2: Ratio becomes 80:120
Step 3: HCF of 80 and 120 = 40
Step 4: 80÷40 : 120÷40 = 2:3
80 cm : 1.2 m = 2:3
Answer 4
Given ratio: flour:sugar = 4:3
I use 200g flour, need to find sugar amount
Step 1: Find the multiplier from 4 to 200
200 ÷ 4 = 50
Step 2: Multiply the sugar part by the same amount
Sugar needed = 3 × 50 = 150g
Step 3: Check the ratio: 200:150 = 4:3 ✓
I need 150g of sugar
Answer 5
Ratio Anna:Ben:Charlie = 2:3:5
Total to share = £60
Step 1: Add ratio parts: 2 + 3 + 5 = 10 parts total
Step 2: Find value of 1 part: £60 ÷ 10 = £6
Step 3: Calculate each share:
Anna gets: 2 × £6 = £12
Ben gets: 3 × £6 = £18
Charlie gets: 5 × £6 = £30
Step 4: Check: £12 + £18 + £30 = £60 ✓
Anna: £12, Ben: £18, Charlie: £30
Answer 6
Ratio of angles = 2:3:4
Sum of angles in triangle = 180°
Step 1: Add ratio parts: 2 + 3 + 4 = 9 parts
Step 2: Find value of 1 part: 180° ÷ 9 = 20°
Step 3: Calculate each angle:
First angle: 2 × 20° = 40°
Second angle: 3 × 20° = 60°
Third angle: 4 × 20° = 80°
Step 4: Check: 40° + 60° + 80° = 180° ✓
The angles are 40°, 60°, and 80°
Answer 7
Ratio boys:girls = 3:4
Total students = 28
Step 1: Add ratio parts: 3 + 4 = 7 parts
Step 2: Find value of 1 part: 28 ÷ 7 = 4 students
Step 3: Calculate numbers:
Boys: 3 × 4 = 12 students
Girls: 4 × 4 = 16 students
Step 4: Check: 12 + 16 = 28 ✓
There are 12 boys and 16 girls
Answer 8
Ratio red:blue:yellow = 5:3:2
Total paint needed = 2 litres
Step 1: Add ratio parts: 5 + 3 + 2 = 10 parts
Step 2: Find value of 1 part: 2 ÷ 10 = 0.2 litres
Step 3: Calculate each color:
Red paint: 5 × 0.2 = 1.0 litre
Blue paint: 3 × 0.2 = 0.6 litres
Yellow paint: 2 × 0.2 = 0.4 litres
Step 4: Check: 1.0 + 0.6 + 0.4 = 2.0 litres ✓
Need 1.0L red, 0.6L blue, 0.4L yellow paint

Topic 4: Solving Linear Equations

Practice Problems & Detailed Answer Guide

Key Concepts to Remember

Balance method: Whatever you do to one side, do to the other side

Inverse operations: +/- are inverses, ×/÷ are inverses

Order: Undo addition/subtraction first, then multiplication/division

Check: Substitute your answer back into the original equation

Problem 1
Solve these equations:
a) x + 7 = 12
b) y - 5 = -3
c) 3z = 21
d) w ÷ 4 = 6
Problem 2
Solve these equations:
a) 2x + 5 = 17
b) 3y - 8 = 10
c) 4z + 3 = -5
Problem 3
Solve these equations with unknowns on both sides:
a) 5x + 2 = 3x + 8
b) 7y - 4 = 2y + 11
c) 4z - 7 = z + 5
Problem 4
Solve these equations involving brackets:
a) 3(x + 2) = 18
b) 2(y - 4) = 10
c) 5(2z + 1) = 25
Problem 5
Solve these equations with fractions:
a) x/3 = 5
b) (y + 2)/4 = 3
c) (2z - 1)/5 = 2
Problem 6
Solve these more complex equations:
a) 2(x + 3) = x + 10
b) 3(y - 2) = 2y + 1
c) 4(z + 1) = 2(z + 8)
Problem 7
Solve and check your answers:
a) 5x - 3 = 2x + 9
b) 3(y + 4) = 2(y + 7)
Problem 8
Solve these equations with negative solutions:
a) 2x + 8 = 0
b) 3y - 6 = -15
c) 4z + 12 = z - 3

Detailed Answer Guide - Topic 4

Answer 1
a) x + 7 = 12
Subtract 7 from both sides: x = 12 - 7 = 5
Check: 5 + 7 = 12 ✓
x = 5
b) y - 5 = -3
Add 5 to both sides: y = -3 + 5 = 2
Check: 2 - 5 = -3 ✓
y = 2
c) 3z = 21
Divide both sides by 3: z = 21 ÷ 3 = 7
Check: 3 × 7 = 21 ✓
z = 7
d) w ÷ 4 = 6
Multiply both sides by 4: w = 6 × 4 = 24
Check: 24 ÷ 4 = 6 ✓
w = 24
Answer 2
a) 2x + 5 = 17
Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides: 2x = 17 - 5 = 12
Step 2: Divide both sides by 2: x = 12 ÷ 2 = 6
Check: 2(6) + 5 = 12 + 5 = 17 ✓
x = 6
b) 3y - 8 = 10
Step 1: Add 8 to both sides: 3y = 10 + 8 = 18
Step 2: Divide both sides by 3: y = 18 ÷ 3 = 6
Check: 3(6) - 8 = 18 - 8 = 10 ✓
y = 6
c) 4z + 3 = -5
Step 1: Subtract 3 from both sides: 4z = -5 - 3 = -8
Step 2: Divide both sides by 4: z = -8 ÷ 4 = -2
Check: 4(-2) + 3 = -8 + 3 = -5 ✓
z = -2
Answer 3
a) 5x + 2 = 3x + 8
Step 1: Subtract 3x from both sides: 5x - 3x + 2 = 8
Step 2: Simplify: 2x + 2 = 8
Step 3: Subtract 2: 2x = 6
Step 4: Divide by 2: x = 3
Check: 5(3) + 2 = 17, 3(3) + 8 = 17 ✓
x = 3
b) 7y - 4 = 2y + 11
Step 1: Subtract 2y: 5y - 4 = 11
Step 2: Add 4: 5y = 15
Step 3: Divide by 5: y = 3
y = 3
c) 4z - 7 = z + 5
Step 1: Subtract z: 3z - 7 = 5
Step 2: Add 7: 3z = 12
Step 3: Divide by 3: z = 4
z = 4
Answer 4
a) 3(x + 2) = 18
Method 1 - Expand first: 3x + 6 = 18, so 3x = 12, so x = 4
Method 2 - Divide first: (x + 2) = 6, so x = 4
x = 4
b) 2(y - 4) = 10
Divide both sides by 2: y - 4 = 5
Add 4: y = 9
y = 9
c) 5(2z + 1) = 25
Divide by 5: 2z + 1 = 5
Subtract 1: 2z = 4
Divide by 2: z = 2
z = 2

b) (y + 2)/4 = 3

Common Mistakes to Avoid:

• Don't forget to change signs when moving terms across the equals sign
• Always do the same operation to both sides
• Be careful with negative numbers - check your arithmetic
• Always substitute back to check your answer
